Community Statement

The community of Tezos supporters is critical to the success of the Tezos protocol as well as the Tezos Foundation. As a unified voice of developers, community organizers, website resource creators, delegator services, contributors, and others involved we want to make sure that our voice is heard. The collective voice of the community.

We request the removal of Johann Gevers from the foundation. Independent of the results of the audit, the foundation needs another leader. There is too much distrust of Gevers within the community and negative publicity outside of the community. This is due to both internal factors in how Gevers has handled the foundation and external factors with another recently failed venture, Monetas. The trust of Gevers within the community cannot be salvaged at this point.

The concerns about Gevers from the community include but are not limited to: lack of communication, delays in providing resources to development work, delays in organizing a coherent PR effort, delays in marketing efforts, delays in overall building of the foundation's organization that is needed to accomplish its mission, inability to resolve disputes between the Foundation and DLS, lack of experience running an almost $1B institution, and speaking to the press first without ever attempting to speak with the community directly, either before or after trouble surfaced.
